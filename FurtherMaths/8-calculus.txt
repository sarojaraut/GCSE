Gradient of a straight line is a measure of the steepness
    The calculation is the "change in y" divided by the "change in x". 
    A positive gradient means the line is increasing from left to right. 
    A negative gradient means the line is decreasing from left to right. 

    It can refer to the rate at which a quantity like temperature, pressure, or concentration changes over a distance. 



Gradient of a curve
    we can draw tangents at a point on curve and calculate tangents slope or use differentiation

    Differentiation is used to find the gradient of a tangent to a curve.
    While the gradient of a straight line is constant, for a curve, the gradient is always changing.
    The gradient of a curve at a given point is defined as being the gradient of the tangent at that point.
    The function that tells us the gradient of the tangent and curve at the given point is called the derivative.
    The formal technique for finding the gradient of a tangent is known as differentiation from first principles and is written as:
    f`(x)  = [ f (x + h) - f (x) ] /h [ as  (x+h)-x = h], 
        where h is the horizontal distance between two points on the curve and has a limit tending to zero.

    Using differentiation from first principles, differentiate f(x) = x**2 
        => F`(x)  = [(x+h)**2 - x**2]/h 
                    = 2x + h As h tends to zero, the derivative f`(x)=2x

    Differentiation is used in maths for calculating rates of change.

    For example in mechanics, the rate of change of displacement (with respect to time) is the velocity.
    The rate of change of velocity (with respect to time) is the acceleration.
